a bend in space and time

  1. The video could be projected as a live set changing simultaneously with the surrounding music.
  2. The two sets of visuals should never be repeated on top of each other.
  3. All footage must represent instances where time is either moving slower or faster.

The video essentially has two layers, the top layer is made up of four distinct visuals. The four visuals were made using processing, using 50 straight lines and a few lines of code using mathematical references. The computer generated endless visuals that one could stay looking at for hours. I chose four out of the number of videos that I generated and created four respective prints for each video. The idea was that if you had to have the four prints successively flashing one after the other and then projecting the video itself on them, the moving visuals in the video would at some point meet the static stills on the prints, thus creating an instance where if time was stopped at that specific moment you would have joined something moving with something stationary. The lower layer consists of a blend of footage which I have collected throughout, footage that describes itself once seen, clips where time effectively changes its speed. All the footage was combined and organised on Resolume. When played with the settings chosen, the video is consistently changing in a random order taking into account any sound that it catches through the microphone. Soundtrack “Christian Vogel - Cancion Sintecta” from the movie “Enter the Void” by Gaspar Noe. The video itself is 6 minutes and 17 seconds long, the double exposure of the real-life footage is never once repeated.
